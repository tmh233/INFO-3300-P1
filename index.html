How big is the musical 1%?

The billboard Hot-100 features 5200 different songs in a year. How many different songs "make it", however? The overal trend is ever less big-name-artists dominate the charts.

On the left, 56 years of billboard data on unique song/artist counts per year.

Note how...

  • ... the 60s saw the most fluent charts. In '67, 827 different songs made it. That's more than double the measly 406 charted in 1996.
  • ... 1970 had 423 different top performers; last year it was only 346.
  • ... the 90s were the decade of a few mainstream bangers. Only 291 different artists charted in '92; an all-time low.
  • ... there's 52 weeks/year with 100 chart positions each - 5200 total positions per year. This means in the early '00s, only about 10% of a week's chart was new material.

Artist Revenue vs. Popularity (Spotify)

Popularity doesn't always translate into revenue; at least not by the Spotify metrics. The size of the circle reflects the number of followers the artist has on Spotify and the brightness of the circles indicates the amount of revenue earned.

Note how...

  • ... there seems to be a bit of a generational gap at aroudn 2.5M followers. Except for Jay Z Lady Gaga and Justin Timberlake, all top-grossing arists below 2.5M followers are... "classics".
  • ... at about the same 4.0M followers, Katy Perry earned more than x3 as much as Bruno Mars, 135M vs 4M.
  • ... Dr.Dre and Maroon 5 are tied at "the bottom of the top" in terms of earnings, in spite of one having over x6.5 more followers than the other.
